Apple Cider

A creative shortcut dessert that transforms Hawaiian rolls into apple cider-filled donuts with a spiced sugar coating.

These warm, sweet treats capture all the cozy flavors of autumn in an easy-to-make package.

Ingredients

  • 1 (12-count) package Hawaiian rolls
  • 1 cup apple pie filling
  • 2 (0.74 ounce) pouches apple cider powdered drink mix (such as Alpine Spiced Apple Cider®)
  • 5 tablespoons butter, divided
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Prepare a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.
  2. Prepare the rolls:Separate the Hawaiian rolls and use a small knife to cut a 1/2-inch slit in the side of each one. Gently widen the opening with your finger or the end of a spoon to create a pocket large enough to hold about 1 tablespoon of filling.
  3. Make the filling:Roughly chop the apple pie filling into smaller pieces and place in a bowl. Stir in one packet of the apple cider drink mix until well combined.
  4. Fill the rolls:Carefully spoon about 1 tablespoon of the apple filling mixture into each prepared roll, ensuring the pocket is filled but not overstuffed.
  5. Coat with butter:Melt 4 tablespoons of butter and brush each roll generously on all sides with the melted butter. Place the filled rolls on the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Bake:Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 12-14 minutes, or until the rolls are lightly toasted and golden brown.
  7. Prepare the coating:While the rolls are baking, stir together the remaining packet of apple cider mix, sugar, and cinnamon in a shallow dish or plate. Melt the remaining 1 tablespoon of butter.
  8. Coat the warm rolls:Once the rolls are out of the oven, immediately brush the tops with the remaining melted butter. While still warm, carefully roll each bun in the cinnamon-sugar mixture until evenly coated.
  9. Serve:Serve warm for the best flavor and texture. These are best enjoyed the same day they're made.
